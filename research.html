Research Interests

My research focuses on AI Chip and System, LLM algorithms, with the following directions:

  • Computing-in-Memory chips for Emerging AI applications
  • AI accelerators for LLM applications
  • AI infrastructure/system for LLM tasks
  • Applications of LLMs

I have an AI chip-related paper collection at Neural Networks on Silicon, which is helpful for students to learn the history and SOTA of AI chip and system research.

Computing-in-Memory chips for Emerging AI applications

Here, I mainly present the Research on RRAM-based in-memory computing chips for AI applications that I conducted during my Ph.D.

AI accelerators for LLM applications

Due to the capabilities currently achieved by LLMs, the exploration and design of chip architectures for LLMs have become extremely critical. Here, we will focus on introducing existing chip architectures from academia and industry, as well as my own thoughts and explorations on these architectures.

AI infrastructure/system for LLM tasks

Supported by the scaling law, the scale of LLMs is continuously expanding. A single chip cannot support the inference and training tasks of a full LLM. The importance of AI systems is increasing. Here, we will explore the important related technologies of AI systems.

Applications of LLMs

Here, I will introduce my research on LLM algorithms, like sparse and mixed precision computing. I will also introduce some good ideas about the applications of LLMs to support future entrepreneurship.
